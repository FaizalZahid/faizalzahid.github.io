Reason for Living

29th August 2024

Assalamualaikum and peace be upon you!

Thursday. Continuing the same unit from yesterday. Reason for living. Why do you think the title is called that? It's because of this:

One of the words practiced today! Let's see kanji learned today:

  • 遊ぶ (asobu) = to play
  • 涙😢 (namida) = tears
  • 生きがい (ikigai) = reason for living
  • 流す (nagasu) = to shed
  • 眺める (nagameru) = to look at
So are your reason for living to play, to look around and finally to shed tears? LOL, so random. 🧸➡🙈➡😢

P/S: Ikigai is actually a title of a popular book:

生き甲斐 - The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life
I guess that is how you write the kanji for がい (gai).

I recommend giving this book a try for those who are into longevity, healthy lifestyle and who are often stressful. By the way,

what is your reason for living?

For Muslims, the cliche one but nevertheless true is:

"I did not create jinn and humans except to worship Me." — 51:56

But some people, especially non-Muslims, might think that that is mundane and lame. Because the act of worshipping is associated with the imageries of rituals and ceremonies, right?

The term 'ibadah' (from Arabic) in Islam doesn't actually constrict it to rituals but

including keeping speech free "from filth, falsehood, malice, abuse", and dishonesty, obeying Islamic Shariah law in "commercial and economic affairs" and in "dealings with your parents, relatives, friends", and everyone else.

Even the boring act of our daily work can be an ibadah if the intention is for God, for providing yourself and family (because that is what God wants you to do)

This can be a long entry because it's such a deep philosophical question, right?

"What wakes you up in the morning?"
"What drives you?"
"What is your motivation?"

So I guess we'll leave it there for self-reflection.
